Center for Applied Research and Development

Established on April 15,1994 CARD is a part of the collaboration program between Institute of Engineering /Tribhuvan University and the Norwegian University of Science and Technology, University of Trondheim through funds made available by Norwegian Universities' Committee for Development Research and Education (NUFU). Its is an autonomous research and development center within the IOE, Tribhuvan University (TU). CARD thrives to establish a culture of research and development leadership at the IOE. It shall support all kinds of scholarly activities: fundamental research, applied research development, contract research to government, non-governmental and international agencies. It capitalizes on the wide range of multi-disciplinary in-house expertise and capabilities of the IOE.

History of IOE ROBOCON

Institute of Engineering (I.O.E) has been organizing different ROBOTICS events in the past. National Robotic Competitions (Robocup Nepal 6 times and IOE robocon 1 time on different themes), workshop on Robotics and Automation were the few events that were helpful to the Robotics enthusiasts. This year Center for Applied Research and Development (CARD) of Institute of Engineering (I.O.E) is teaming up with Robotics Club, Pulchowk Campus to organize a National Robotics Competition. This year the theme for Robotic Contest is "Ujyalo Nepal". The contest shall take place between the undergraduate students of college/university/polytechnic of the nation.

1.Robo-Soccer 2004



Theme:


The theme of the contest was Soccer, as the name depicts. Each colleges had to have 3 students operating the robot and one team leader and instructor. The robots had to be remote-controlled. 16 matches were played, each between two teams such that four colleges had to play two matches in the group stage. The competition took place in four stages, three knockout rounds after the group stage. A total of 19 games were to be played. The game was played in a 4m X 2m field containing 11 tennis balls. The students needed to drive the robots to chase the balls and score them into goal posts amidst music, played in energizing full volume. Each goal would be awarded 10 points. The team scoring the most number of goals within the defined time limit would be declared the winner.

2. Robo-Shooter 2005



Theme:


The theme of the competition was to shoot down the opponents pins by tennis balls within the defined time limit (or in the shortest possible time). Each team consisted of an instructor, three playing members and a desired member of the pit crew. There would be one manual robot necessarily and one (or optionally more) automated machines. The manual machine and the automated machines all had to fit within the size limits of 50cm x 50cm x 75cm and 40cm x 40cm x 40cm respectively. Moreover the whole equipment could not exceed 35 kg. The manual machine of each team would be provided with 7 green balls whereas the automated machine would be provided with 11 red tennis balls with which they had to topple down as many of the opponents’ pins as possible within the period of 3 minutes. One would be the blue team with blue pins and the other would be the red team possessing red pins. The pins were 45 cm tall each and 11 in number for each team.
The automated machines of both the teams were supposed to follow the green belts, while the manual were to be guided through the red and blue belts drawn respectively on the either sides of the pin-zone. Both the automated and the manual machines had to target the countercolored pins that stood on the opposition’s side and each toppling of those pins would earn them 10 points. In addition to that, a special golden pin was also placed in the center that would help them score an alluring 25. However, if any side toppled their own pins, each pin would cost them 10 points. Similar deduction would occur if the robots crossed their zones or toppled the opponent’s pins through physical contact.
A team would automatically win if any one of its robots toppled down all of the opponent’s pins first before the time completed. If not, the winner would be decided based on the points achieved in 3 minutes.
15 teams that participated in the contest were divided into 5 groups of three each and the teams of each group would compete with the two others in round robin format. Along with the 5 group winners, 3 top scoring teams from the pool of rest 10 teams would advance to the knockout stage (quarter final).

3. ROBOCUP 2006



Theme:


The theme of the contest was ‘Mission Everest’ and as the theme suggested, the objective was to simulate the climbing of Mount Everest and reaching its summit by a group of robots while transporting supplies to the camp and more notably, cleaning the trail up the summit. Each team would have to make one manual and one (or optionally more) automated robots. The manual robot had to fit in the dimensions 1.5m x 1m x 0.8m whereas each of the automated robots had to be within the size 75m x 75m x 75m. The total weight of the equipment couldn’t exceed 50 kg. Each team could have 3 students, 1 instructor and an optional technical assistant.
The game field was of dimensions 10m x 9m and five transparent cylindrical baskets were kept in the area depicting the base camp. The rubbish and trash were substituted by tin or plastic jars. Similarly, the provisions were symbolized by balls. The manual robot had to collect the waste materials and dump them in the dump zone while the automated machine had to put their team’s balls in at least 5 transparent cylinders placed in the game field and were also assigned to put the teams’ flag in the summit zone. The manual and automated robots move in their specified zones only.
Each waste collection would fetch 2 points while putting the balls in the outside and inside cylinders would get them 5 points and 10 points respectively. Finally, the first team to put the flag in the summit zone would get 20 points and the second one would get 15 points. If a team succeeded in dropping at least 3 balls in 3 collinear cylinders, dumping at least one waste in dumping zone and putting the flag at the summit before 3 minutes ended, the team would be the ‘Everest Conqueror’ and automatically win that game.
The contest would be played by dividing the teams into groups and making the teams of each group compete in round robin league format, based on which the eight excelling teams would advance to the knockout stage.

NCIT and Khwopa were hence bound to be the 2nd Runners Up.
The crowd of 1900 was eager to see whether KEC would successfully defend their title or would there be a new champion that year. Advanced did their best but their efforts weren’t enough to beat their rivals and had to be satisfied in the 1st Runner Up position as KEC retained the Grand Prix.

4.Bhoto Jatra 2008


Theme:


The theme of the 4th National Robot Contest was “Bhoto Jatra”, which commemorated the chariot procession and the auspicious displaying of the bejeweled waistcoat, the Bhoto. The game involved building a chariot (Rath) on an automatic machine machine with the help of a manual machine. Both teams had to fill up a centrally located zone symbolizing the Bhoto. Team were awarded points for different tasks performed by both the robots. Each game lasted for 3 minutes and the team collecting maximum points was declared the winner.

5. ROBOCUP 2010



Theme:


The theme of Robocup 2010 was “Working together for better Nepal”. The slogan was chosen to promote a strong sense of helpfulness and co-operation among people, to make Nepal better and to fortify the feeling of unity, fair play and fair competition among the technical students of our country. The game consisted of a pre-designed game field. There were white stripes on the green background which guided the robots to find their path. Each team made two robots, one manual operated by the operator and another automatic consisting of a chip and sensors which would lead the robot along the white stripes.

The robots were the symbols of “driver of the nation”, working together to complete a set of tasks to build a prosperous nation. The automatic robot received a “token of success” from manual robot and climbed up and downhill while the manual robot bridged the “broken trail”. On its way, the automatic robot gives the three scoring balls, representing child, youth and elderly people of the society, to the manual robots. The manual robot then would take the three scoring balls to the “prosperous society”. The game would complete once the automatic robot scored the “token of success” on the “chest of glory”.

6. Robocup 2012



Theme:


The theme for this edition of the Contest was “Clean Bagmati, Green Kathmandu“. This theme emulates the need and the feeling of co-ordination among the robots to clean the wastes in the Bagmati river and make the Kathmandu area more Greener. The robots works together for the Cleanliness and Greenery to signify the brighter future of Nepal.
The Manual Robot classifies the wastes into corresponding Degradable/ Non Degradable region. The Automatic Robot carries degradable waste into manure production zone to produce the manure. The Manual Robot carried manure and also the tree trunk into tree plantation zone. Finally, Automatic Robot carried tree cap onto tree plantation zone for successful Plantation. Each match is contested by Red and Blue teams and lasts for three minutes. The team scoring more number of points won the game.

7. IOE Robocon 2016



Theme:


Our country Nepal has the most varying geographical terrains in the whole world. The terrains vary from flat belts to mid hills to very steep mountain ranges. Despite the awkward and astonishing terrains, people reside in flat belts as well as near steep mountains. Although the lifestyle of people in southern flat regions has significantly improved, there still exists hardship in the hilly and mountain regions. People still transport consumable and non-consumable goods from Terai region to Hills and Mountains for sustaining their lives. People face various obstacles like flood, landslides, etc. during the transport.
Based on the facts above, the game of IOE Robocon 2016 is designed in order to visualize the geographical terrains of our country and transport of various items from flat belts to high mountains. Each team has to build one robot. The robot picks two payloads from flat belt. One is common consumable good (salt) and other is a telecommunication device. Crossing the different terrains and removing the various obstacles, the telecommunication device is dropped off at Pokhara and the consumable good is dropped off at Mustang. So, the theme of the contest “Journey to Mustang”.

A match is contested by Red and Blue teams. It lasts 3 minutes at most. Each team consists of one robot. Robots start from Robot Start Zone and pick up the payloads from the Vandar. Then the robots run along three zones; Terai, Hills, Mountains, and aim for Mustang.
Robot starts from the Robot Start Zone and picks up two payloads, a telecommunication device and salt from the Vandar. Then it climbs the Hill and reaches the Valley where it drops the first payload i.e. the telecommunication device at Pokhara. Clearing the obstacles from its path, the robot aims towards Mountain. With the help of Tuin, robot moves from Tarr to Mustang. The robot drops the second payload i.e. salt at Mustang. The team that successfully delivers payloads earlier is the winner of the game. This type of winning is called “Sahi-Ho”.
